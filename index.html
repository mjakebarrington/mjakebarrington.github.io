About Me

I am an aspiring software engineer in the Burlington, VT area. My main area of interest is in data science, machine learning, and artificial intelligence. I attended the University of Vermont from 2014 through 2016 where I majored in Biology. After realizing that biology wasn't my cup of tea and that I didn't know exactly what I wanted to study I left university and started working in the trades.

After learning the value of hard work in the trades I decided to continue my education without formally reentering university. I started taking mathematics courses through Community College of Vermont. During that time I was introduced to data science careers while taking a statistics class. I also taught myself Python in order to use the SymPy symbolic computation ("computer algebra") library.

In the summer of 2019 a family member passed away and left my family with funds to be used on the condition that they be used for personal development/education. I used some of the money to enroll in a web development bootcamp at the Burlington Code Academy. There I learned JavaScript, MongoDB, HTML5, CSS3, React, and Node. My group's capstone project involved building an "action" for Google Assistant that allows users to update and inquire about their "You Need A Budget" accounts. Most of the project was done using Google's firebase and dialogflow platforms, but I helped write a "custom function" which can be found on github.

Since finishing the web development bootcamp I have been teaching myself linear algebra, taking a data science course through UMich on coursera, and plan on taking a machine learning course (also through coursera). My longer term goals include taking a course on deep learning, finishing my degree, and potentially going to graduate school. For now, I'm looking for employment and interesting projects to work on. In my free time I enjoy skiing, snowboarding, craft beer, and spending time with my daughter.
