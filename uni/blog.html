Self Teaching

In this day and age information is available virtually instantly at the click of a button.

Now, I find it prudent to defend self-teaching. In it's vastness the web offers many criticisms of the endeveor. In this section I will attempt to counter many such criticisms. I will make every attempt to be as objective as possible.

Lecture and text don't always parallel

This may be the easiest to debunk. While it's true that lectures often provide supplemental knowledge not available in a textbook or a course text, the internet offers vast amounts of recorded lectures. This is why in my curriculum I focus on a healthy mix of reading materials and recorded lectures. Nearly all computer science courses at MIT, for instance, have recorded lectures available at MIT OpenCourseWare. If a course doesn't happen to have recorded lectures available, another quality institution likely will. OpenCourser does a good job indexing many of them, among other sites.
